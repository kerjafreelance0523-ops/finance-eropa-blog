## Bitcoin quantum threat: what to harden

The **Bitcoin quantum threat** is focused on UTXOs where public keys are exposed. In the P2PK vs P2PKH distinction, Pay-to-Public-Key outputs are permanently exposed; CoinShares quantum research and estimates of 4 million BTC exposed define the target set. The Satoshi 1M BTC wallets are the most famous example of the Bitcoin P2PK vulnerability; a mempool 10-minute attack remains theoretical.

## Practical quantum safety checklist

First, stop reusing addresses. Every reuse re-exposes the same public key. Use a fresh address for each payment; prefer exchanges that avoid or minimise reuse. Second, move coins off truly legacy formats. If you hold very old BTC, some funds may sit in Pay-to-Public-Key outputs. Moving them into P2PKH, P2SH, or SegWit improves your quantum posture. Third, choose reputable, actively maintained wallets and custodians. Fourth, diversify storage and stay informed. Do not keep all BTC in a single wallet; follow balanced research from CoinShares so you know when major quantum or post-quantum milestones occur. By turning these habits into routine, you turn the Bitcoin quantum threat from a buzzword into a manageable part of your long-term strategy. The same analytical discipline used to assess ECB interest rates 2026 and eurozone inflation 1.9% should extend to operational security: focus on what you can control, and stay informed without reacting to every headline.
