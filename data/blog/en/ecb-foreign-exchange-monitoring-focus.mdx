## Eurozone inflation forecast 1.9% and the FX channel

The ECB’s mandate is price stability, with inflation targeted at 2% over the medium term. Staff projections show eurozone inflation 1.9% in the forecast horizon, supported by moderating energy costs and eurozone GDP growth 1.2%. Exchange rates influence that path: a stronger euro lowers import prices and helps dampen inflation; a weaker euro can push inflation up. The ECB deposit rate 2.00% and the corridor around main refinancing operations are set to be mildly restrictive; the level of the euro then determines how quickly that stance feeds through to prices. For ECB interest rates 2026, the Governing Council does not target the exchange rate, but it cannot ignore it when assessing the outlook for eurozone inflation 1.9%.

The deposit facility rate and main refinancing operations form the core of the framework. When the euro appreciates, dollar-denominated energy and other imports become cheaper in euro terms, which can ease headline inflation. When the euro depreciates, the opposite holds. ## FX transmission and policy implications

Exchange rate effects take time to filter through the economy. Impact on import prices can be relatively quick; effects on export competitiveness and eurozone GDP growth 1.2% may take longer. This lag complicates the timing of monetary policy. The ECB’s statement that it is monitoring FX trends does not mean it is targeting the exchange rate—that would fall outside its mandate. It does mean that currency movements are an important input to inflation and growth forecasts, and thus to decisions on the deposit facility rate and main refinancing operations.

For investors, this signals a more holistic view of monetary conditions. The ECB is considering the full set of factors that affect eurozone inflation 1.9% and growth, including the Fed vs ECB comparison and global capital flows. The quantitative easing pause and subsequent normalisation have left a clearer rate structure; the level of ECB interest rates 2026 and the path of the euro will continue to shape the DAX 40 forecast and broader asset prices. The EU monetary policy outlook remains data-dependent, with FX as one of the key data inputs.

## EU monetary policy outlook 2026

Over the coming year, the ECB is likely to keep the deposit facility rate near 2.00% while monitoring inflation, growth, and global developments—including FX. If eurozone inflation 1.9% settles durably around or below target and growth softens, gradual rate cuts could be considered; if inflation proves stickier or the euro weakens sharply, the corridor could be held or raised. The 2% goal and a flexible, meeting-by-meeting approach will continue to define the EU monetary policy outlook.
