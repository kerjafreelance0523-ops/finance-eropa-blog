In 2026, most serious trading and withdrawals on MEXC require a verified account.

Your data is processed according to MEXC’s privacy policy and is used for verification and fraud prevention. Verification usually takes from a few minutes to a few hours, depending on queue and document quality; ensure your ID is clear and all four corners are visible to avoid delays.

## How to Complete KYC on MEXC Step by Step

Log in to your MEXC account and go to the account or security section. Look for “Identity verification”, “KYC”, or “Verify now”. Choose the verification level that matches your needs; MEXC often has basic and advanced tiers with different limits. Select your country and document type (passport or ID card). ## Why 2FA Is Non-Negotiable for a Safe Account

Two-factor authentication (2FA) means a second check besides your password. When you log in or perform sensitive actions (e.g. withdraw), MEXC asks for a code from your phone: from an authenticator app (such as Google Authenticator or Authy) or via SMS. Even if someone steals or guesses your password, they cannot pass this step without your device. In 2026, account takeovers and phishing are still common; 2FA is one of the most effective defences. MEXC supports both app-based and SMS 2FA; app-based is generally more secure because SMS can be intercepted in some cases.

Enable 2FA as soon as your account is created. Go to security or account settings, find “Two-factor authentication” or “2FA”, and follow the steps. You will scan a QR code with your authenticator app or register your phone number for SMS. Save the backup codes in a safe place; they let you recover access if you lose your phone. Do not share 2FA codes or backup codes with anyone; MEXC will never ask for them.
