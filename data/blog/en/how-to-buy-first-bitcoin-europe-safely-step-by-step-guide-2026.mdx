## Why EU regulation makes buying Bitcoin safer

The EU’s Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) framework and national rules require licensed exchanges to follow know-your-customer (KYC) and anti-money-laundering (AML) checks. That means the platform must verify your identity and report suspicious activity. For you, it reduces the risk of dealing with unlicensed or scam operators. Exchanges that serve European customers with euro deposits typically support SEPA transfers, which are cheap and traceable, and many also accept card payments for smaller amounts. Understanding that regulation protects you can make the first step feel less daunting.

KYC usually means uploading a government-issued ID and sometimes a selfie. The process is standard across major platforms and typically takes from a few minutes to a couple of days. Once verified, you get higher deposit and withdrawal limits and full access to buying and selling. Treat KYC as a one-time setup that unlocks a safer path to your first Bitcoin purchase.

## Steps 2–5: Account, verification, deposit, buy, and security

**Step 2 – Create an account and verify your identity.** Sign up with your email or phone and a strong password. Enter any referral code at sign-up if you have one, so you can get the fee discount. Then go to the verification section and upload your ID and, if asked, a selfie. Most platforms approve within 24–48 hours. Until then, you may have limited deposit or withdrawal limits, but you can often already explore the interface and see how buying works.

**Step 3 – Deposit euros.** Once verified, add euros via SEPA bank transfer (usually free or low cost, one to three business days) or by card (faster but often with a small fee). Use only the official bank details or card flow shown in your exchange account; never send euros to an address or account you received by email or message. A first deposit of €100 is enough to buy a small amount of BTC and get used to the process.

**Step 4 – Buy your first BTC or ETH.** In the exchange app or website, go to “Buy crypto” or “Trade”, choose the EUR pair (e.g. BTC/EUR or ETH/EUR), enter the amount in euros (e.g. €50 or €100), and confirm. You now own a small amount of Bitcoin or Ethereum held in your exchange account. You can leave it there while you learn, or later move a portion to your own wallet if you decide to hold long term.

**Step 5 – Basic security.** Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your account as soon as possible. Use an authenticator app rather than SMS if the option exists. Never share your password, 2FA codes, or (if you later use your own wallet) your seed phrase with anyone. Exchanges and legitimate support will never ask for these. These habits protect you from the most common account takeovers and phishing.
